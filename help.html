Common questions & troubleshooting

Audio & Sound

The most common cause is silent mode.

  • iPhone: Find the small physical switch on the upper-left side of your phone. Flip it so no orange is showing — that means your phone is in Ring mode.
  • Android: Press the volume-up button on the side of your phone. Make sure the media volume (not just ringtone) is turned up.

If you're already off silent mode, also try connecting headphones — they bypass the phone's built-in speaker and often produce much clearer sound.

Wind Instruments & Microphone

Microphone sensitivity varies a lot between phones and devices. If your mic isn't responding, try these tips:

  • Hold the phone closer to your mouth — the mic is usually at the bottom edge.
  • Blow a little more firmly into the microphone opening.
  • Make sure you've granted microphone permission when the browser asked.
Still not working? Use the Hold to Blow button! Press and hold it to produce the wind sound, then — while still holding — tap the numbered hole buttons to change the pitch. You can press any combination of holes at the same time, just like a real wind instrument. Let go of the button to stop the sound.

Each numbered hole corresponds to a specific pitch. The note you hear is determined by which holes you press, not just how many:

  • Lower-numbered holes (① ②) produce lower notes.
  • Higher-numbered holes (④ ⑤) produce higher notes.
  • Pressing multiple holes at once gives a pitch in between — the exact blend depends on which holes you combine.

Experiment! Try holding ① + ③ vs ② + ④ — they'll sound different even though you're pressing the same number of holes.

Playing the Instruments

Mobile: Swipe or drag your finger up and down across the strings. Each string is a different note. You can also tap a single string to pluck it.

Desktop / keyboard: Use number keys 1, 2, 3… to pluck individual strings. You can also click and drag with your mouse.

Mobile: Tap each drum pad or metallophone bar. Each surface produces a different sound.

Desktop / keyboard: Press number keys 1, 2, 3… to hit each pad or bar in order. Try building a repeating rhythm!

  • Tabla: Key 1 = Dayan (high) · Key 2 = Bayan (deep bass)
  • Janggu: Key 1 = 채편 bright crack · Key 2 = 궁편 deep boom
  • Gamelan: Keys 1–5 hit bars from lowest to highest pitch

Yes! Keyboard shortcuts work on all three instrument types:

  • Wind: Number keys 1–5 press/release individual holes. Space bar activates the blow (hold it down, press holes to change pitch).
  • Strings: Number keys 1–8 pluck individual strings.
  • Percussion: Number keys 1–5 hit each pad or bar.

A shortcut reminder is shown at the bottom of every play screen.
